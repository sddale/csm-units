A dimension of a derived quantity. 

Expression of the dependence of a quantity on the base quantities (and their base dimensions) of a system of quantities as a product of powers of factors corresponding to the base quantities, omitting any numerical factors. A power of a factor is the factor raised to an exponent.

A derived dimension can be formed from multiple exponents (i.\+e. speed is represented as \char`\"{}exponent$<$\+L, 1$>$, exponent$<$\+T, -\/1$>$\char`\"{}). It is also possible to form a derived dimension with only one exponent (i.\+e. frequency is represented as just \char`\"{}exponent$<$\+T, -\/1$>$\char`\"{}).
